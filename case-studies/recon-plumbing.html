Mission Brief

Mission

The mission was never “rank a plumbing website.” It was to build a system where real field work compounds into search visibility, brand recall, and procurement-grade trust — without manufacturing content disconnected from what RECON actually does on site.

Every section that follows is one component of that system, in the order it was built.

Business Context

Business Context

RECON Plumbing Services NSW is a commercial and strata plumbing operator based in Sydney, working a roughly one-hour-drive radius from a Georges Hall HQ. The work is reactive by trade — backflow devices fail, boundary traps leak, hot water systems die — but the business model RECON is building toward is recurring: facilities managers, strata operators, and compliance-driven commercial accounts who need a default supplier, not a one-off callout.

That distinction — one-off job vs. default maintenance supplier — is the thread every system below was designed to pull on.

Diagnosis

Diagnosis

The business had no digital memory. Every completed job — the knowledge, the proof, the local relevance — disappeared the moment the van left site.

What was actually happening

Outreach existed but was volume-only. Search presence was effectively invisible (average position outside 40). LinkedIn had an audience forming by accident, not by design. Real expertise — the kind that shows up in a 2am RPZD valve failure — was not being converted into anything reusable.

The root cause

No loop existed connecting operations to marketing. The business was generating proof of expertise every single day and throwing it away. The fix was never “do more SEO” — it was building the pipe that lets field work become search gravity automatically.

Strategic Principles

The doctrine underneath the system.

  1. 01Reality first. Operations are the source of truth — not the content calendar.
  2. 02Operations create marketing. A completed job is raw material, not an afterthought to write up.
  3. 03Marketing creates memory. One job, properly captured, becomes searchable indefinitely.
  4. 04Memory creates trust. Branded search recall is measurable — not assumed.
  5. 05Trust compounds. A default supplier beats a one-off callout, every time.
  6. 06Compounding beats campaigns. This system was never built to spike.

Search Strategy

From invisible to in-contention.

Average search position moved from roughly position 20 to position 15 over a 28-day window — a real, structural improvement, not a fluke spike. Lifetime, the site has produced 639 clicks across 47,814 impressions — a site-wide CTR of 1.34%, against a 2.5–4% category target. That gap is the single fastest lever available: closing it doubles click volume with zero new ranking work.

Brand recall is real and measurable. “recon plumbing services” converts at 39.2% CTR when it appears in search — direct evidence the offline and outreach work is compounding into search behaviour, not just rankings.

Commercial Positioning & Founder Brand

The same reframe, two channels.

On LinkedIn and in outreach framing, the same repositioning move was made: from asking to be hired, to stating readiness to be plugged in.

“Hire us.”

Outbound framing centered on persuasion — competing for attention against every other quote in an inbox.

In parallel, LinkedIn became a procurement-signalling asset, not a vanity channel. The audience composition is the proof: ~21% Real Estate, ~20% Facilities Services, and roughly 57–74% Senior/Manager-level or above across reported windows. Followers grew approximately 30% over the engagement (from roughly 1,143 to 1,748 at points measured). The gap is activation — the audience is strong, posting cadence has been inconsistent.

Dispatch

Dispatch

Every business produces signals. Most disappear the moment the job is done. My work is building the systems that catch them — long enough for a signal to become a competitive advantage instead of a forgotten callout.

I didn't go looking for an SEO win here. I went looking for the leak — the place where real expertise was being produced every day and thrown away. The hardest part was never the search strategy. It was building a loop a non-technical owner could trust without needing to understand it, and reporting on it plainly enough that trust never had to be asked for.

RECON is not a finished case, and I'm not presenting it as one. The Mortlake cluster is a prototype. CTR optimization is a recommendation, not yet a result. I'd rather you see the system mid-flight, honestly labeled, than a polished result I can't fully stand behind.

Transferable Thinking

This was never about plumbing.

This was about diagnosing a business with no digital memory, building the loop that gives it one, and reporting on it with enough discipline that the owner never had to wonder what was happening. I'd run the same diagnosis on any service business with a field layer and an under-used trust asset sitting idle. If that's the problem in front of you — that's the conversation worth having.
